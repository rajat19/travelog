## Why Singapore?

Singapore often gets cast as just a layover destination, but giving it a dedicated few days reveals a city deeply committed to its own reinvention. It's a place where you can eat a $3 Michelin-starred chicken rice dish at a bustling hawker center for lunch, and then walk through an air-conditioned glass dome mimicking a cloud forest for the afternoon.

It’s an incredibly easy introduction to Asia: English is widely spoken, the public transit is flawless, and it's notoriously safe.

## The Rhythm

The city is hyper-efficient. Everything works, and everything is clean. But beneath the slick surface of gleaming malls and Marina Bay Sands lies the true heartbeat of Singapore: the hawker centers.

<Tip title="Hawker Center Chope">
When dining at a hawker center, you might see packets of tissues resting on empty tables. This is "choping"—the local system of reserving a table before you go buy your food. Leave a tissue pack, and the table is yours.
</Tip>

## What Surprised Me

How green it is. Singapore calls itself a "City in a Garden," and they mean it. The roads are lined with massive rain trees, skyscrapers have jungles dripping off their balconies, and places like Gardens by the Bay manage to blend horticulture with science fiction.

## Planning Notes

It is notoriously expensive for alcohol and accommodation, but incredibly cheap for food (if you stick to local spots) and public transport. 

<Highlight title="Singapore Essentials">
- **The Heat**: It is consistently hot and very humid year-round. Plan outdoor activities for the morning or evening, and use the extensive network of air-conditioned malls to navigate the city in the afternoon.
- **Strict Laws**: The fines are real. Do not eat or drink on the MRT, chewing gum is banned, and jaywalking can result in a hefty ticket.
</Highlight>
