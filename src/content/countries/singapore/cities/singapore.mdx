## A City in a Garden

> It is rare to find a city that feels both fiercely modern and deeply rooted in its culinary traditions. 

Singapore is a masterclass in urban planning. It’s a sleek tropical metropolis where skyline views, iconic gardens, legendary food courts, and meticulous design shape everyday travel. From the colonial facades of the Civic District to the narrow lanes of Kampong Glam and the vertical forests of Marina Bay, Singapore is a city constantly looking forward.

<Highlight title="Top Experiences">
- **Gardens by the Bay at night**: Watching the Supertrees light up to music.
- **Hawker Hopping**: Eating your way through Maxwell, Lau Pa Sat, or Tiong Bahru hawker centers.
- **Jewel Changi**: Arriving early for your flight just to stare at the monumental indoor waterfall.
</Highlight>

<Tip title="Paying the Smart Way">
You don't need cash for public transit. Just tap your contactless credit card (Visa/Mastercard) or Apple Pay straight at the MRT gantry. It automatically calculates the fare, no special travel card needed.
</Tip>

## The Supertrees and Cloud Forests

Singapore's waterfront is dominated by Gardens by the Bay. The Cloud Forest dome is spectacular: an artificial mountain covered in lush vegetation and waterfalls, all enclosed in a giant glass climate-controlled dome. Outside, the Supertree Grove looks like something out of Avatar, especially at 7:45 PM and 8:45 PM when they light up for the Garden Rhapsody show.

## Little India & Kampong Glam

Get away from the gleaming malls of Orchard Road to experience Singapore's incredible diversity. Little India is a vibrant explosion of color, flower garland vendors, and the scent of spices. Just a short walk away is Kampong Glam, the historic Muslim quarter centered around the golden dome of the Sultan Mosque, featuring hipster cafes blending seamlessly with traditional fabric shops on Haji Lane.

## The Food Scene

Hawker centers are the lifeblood of Singapore. They are open-air complexes housing dozens (sometimes hundreds) of tiny food stalls, each usually specializing in just one or two dishes.

<Itinerary>
- **Day 1: The Icons** — Gardens by the Bay (Cloud Forest) → Marina Bay Sands → ArtScience Museum → Supertree light show.
- **Day 2: Cultural Quarters** — Breakfast in Chinatown → explore Little India → afternoon in Kampong Glam (Haji Lane).
- **Day 3: Botanical & Historical** — Singapore Botanic Gardens (UNESCO listed) → Orchard Road shopping → evening satay at Lau Pa Sat.
- **Day 4: Island Escape** — Cable car to Sentosa Island for beaches or Universal Studios → Jewel Changi waterfall before departure.
</Itinerary>

## Logistics

<GettingThere>
Changi Airport (SIN) is consistently voted the best in the world. It connects to the city via the MRT in about 40 minutes for roughly $2 SGD. Grab or regular taxis take 20 minutes and cost $20-30 SGD.
</GettingThere>

<Accommodation title="Where I Stayed">
**Chinatown/Tanjong Pagar**: The perfect base. You get incredible localized food right at your doorstep, it's generally slightly cheaper than Marina Bay, and incredibly well-connected on the MRT.
</Accommodation>
