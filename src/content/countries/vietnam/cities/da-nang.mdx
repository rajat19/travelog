## The City of Bridges

> Da Nang feels different from the rest of Vietnam. The streets are wider, the air is saltier, and the chaos is remarkably subdued.

Situated perfectly in Central Vietnam, Da Nang is arguably the country's most livable city. It boasts miles of uncrowded white-sand beaches, lush mountains pushing right up to the coastline, and a skyline defined by spectacular, modern bridges crossing the Han River.

<Highlight title="Da Nang Highlights">
- **The Dragon Bridge**: A massive yellow dragon that literally breathes fire and spits water on weekend nights at 9 PM.
- **Marble Mountains**: A cluster of five marble and limestone hills hiding caves, tunnels, and Buddhist sanctuaries.
- **My Khe Beach**: Once a rest area for GIs during the war, now a pristine stretch of sand perfect for morning runs and surfing.
</Highlight>

<Tip title="The Perfect Basecap">
Da Nang is the ultimate basecamp for Central Vietnam. Rather than staying in Hoi An, stay in Da Nang to enjoy the beaches and modern amenities, and take a 40-minute Grab or scooter ride to Hoi An for the afternoon and evening.
</Tip>

## Central Vietnamese Cuisine

Central Vietnam is famous for its distinct, slightly spicier food compared to the North or South, centered heavily around noodles and fresh seafood.

<FoodGuide>
| Dish | Where to Try | Price |
|------|--------------|-------|
| **Mì Quảng (Turmeric Noodles)** | Mi Quang Ba Mua | ~40k VND |
| **Bún Chả Cá (Fish Cake Noodle)** | Bun Cha Ca 109 | ~35k VND |
| **Fresh Seafood** | Be Man (along the beach) | Varies by weight |
| **Bánh Xèo (Crispy Pancake)** | Ba Duong | ~60k VND |
</FoodGuide>

<Itinerary>
- **Day 1: Coast & Fire** — Relax on My Khe beach → Seafood dinner → Watch the Dragon Bridge breathe fire (weekends only).
- **Day 2: The Mountains** — Climb Marble Mountains in the morning → Drive the spectacular Hai Van Pass (Top Gear famous) by scooter.
- **Day 3: Day Trip to Hoi An** — Head to Hoi An ancient town for the afternoon → Get measured for tailored clothes → See the lantern-lit streets at night.
- **Day 4: Ba Na Hills** — Take the world's longest cable car up past the clouds to walk the famous Golden Bridge held up by giant stone hands.
</Itinerary>

## Logistics

<GettingThere>
Da Nang International Airport (DAD) is incredibly convenient, located practically in the city center. It's a quick 10-15 minute, $5 Grab ride to the beach hotels. It's also a major stop on the North-South "Reunification Express" train.
</GettingThere>

<Accommodation title="Where I Stayed">
**My Khe Beach Area (Son Tra/Ngu Hanh Son)**: The beachfront is lined with modern, affordable hotels. You can get a 4-star hotel with a rooftop infinity pool overlooking the ocean for under $50 a night.
</Accommodation>
