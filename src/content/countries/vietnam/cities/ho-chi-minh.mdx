## The Saigon Hustle

> If you stand still for a moment, you realize the city is a living organism, pulsing with the exhaust and energy of 8 million motorbikes.

Still universally referred to as Saigon by locals, Ho Chi Minh City (HCMC) is the commercial engine of Vietnam. It is loud, hot, and relentlessly energetic. Glossy skyscrapers sit next to crumbling French colonial cathedrals, and narrow alleyways hide some of the best coffee shops you will ever visit.

<Highlight title="HCMC Highlights">
- **War Remnants Museum**: A sobering, essential, and heavily hitting perspective on the Vietnam War.
- **Cafe Culture**: Finding hidden, multi-level cafe apartments (like The Cafe Apartment on Nguyen Hue).
- **Ben Thanh Market**: Navigating the chaotic maze of vendors selling everything from coffee beans to counterfeit sneakers.
</Highlight>

<Tip title="Crossing the Street">
There is an art to crossing the street here: Step off the curb, walk slowly and predictably. Do not stop, do not step backward, do not sprint. The sea of scooters will magically flow around you. Eye contact with drivers helps.
</Tip>

## Colonial Echoes

District 1 is the historical heart of the city. Within a few blocks, you can see the Notre-Dame Cathedral Basilica of Saigon, the stunning Central Post Office (designed by Gustave Eiffel), and the Opera House. It feels distinctly European until you look down and see a woman selling fresh coconuts from a bamboo shoulder pole.

## The Food Scene

Saigon's food is generally sweeter, spicier, and more vibrant than the North. The portions of fresh herbs served with every meal are staggering.

<FoodGuide>
| Dish | Where to Try | Price |
|------|--------------|-------|
| **Cơm Tấm (Broken Rice with Pork)** | Com Tam Ba Ghien | ~70k VND |
| **Bánh Mì** | Banh Mi Huynh Hoa (famous, heavy) | ~60k VND |
| **Phở (Southern Style)** | Pho Hoa Pasteur | ~80k VND |
| **Bún Thịt Nướng (Grilled Pork Noodles)** | Chi Tuyen | ~50k VND |
</FoodGuide>

<Itinerary>
- **Day 1: District 1 History** — Central Post Office & Cathedral → War Remnants Museum → Independence Palace → Sunset drinks at a rooftop bar.
- **Day 2: City Life** — Ben Thanh Market → Nguyen Hue walking street → Cafe hopping in old apartment blocks → Bui Vien walking street (if you want wild nightlife).
- **Day 3: Cu Chi Tunnels** — Half-day trip out of the city to crawl through the incredible Viet Cong tunnel network. 
- **Day 4: Chinatown (Cho Lon)** — Explore District 5 (Binh Tay Market, Thien Hau Temple) for a totally different vibe and great duck noodle soup.
</Itinerary>

## Logistics

<GettingThere>
Tan Son Nhat International Airport (SGN) is busy and chaotic. Book a Grab car from the app upon arrival rather than aggressively negotiating with the taxi touts at the exit.
</GettingThere>

<Accommodation title="Where I Stayed">
**District 1 or District 3**: District 1 is the absolute center, near all the major sights. Stay in District 3 for slightly quieter, leafier streets that are still walking distance to the core action.
</Accommodation>
