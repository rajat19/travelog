## The Pearl of the Orient

Ho Chi Minh City, still called Saigon by locals, is Vietnam at its most intense. Millions of motorbikes weave through streets lined with French colonial architecture, modern skyscrapers, and ancient pagodas. The energy is intoxicating: cyclo drivers dodge luxury cars, coffee drips slowly through phin filters, and the city's complicated history lives on in every district.

## History & Architecture

The Notre-Dame Basilica, built with materials shipped from France in the 1880s, anchors the city center alongside the ornate Central Post Office designed by Gustave Eiffel. The War Remnants Museum is a sobering and essential visit that presents the Vietnam War from the Vietnamese perspective. The Reunification Palace, where a tank crashed through the gates in 1975, remains frozen in time, a monument to the moment that changed the nation.

## Food & Markets

Saigon's food scene is extraordinary. Start your morning with a bowl of pho, the broth simmered for hours and served with fresh herbs and lime. Ben Thanh Market is a sensory explosion where you can haggle for silk, lacquerware, and tropical fruits. After dark, head to Bui Vien Street for the backpacker nightlife, or find a rooftop bar and watch the city's neon skyline twinkle against the Saigon River.
