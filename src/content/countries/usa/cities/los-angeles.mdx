## City of Dreams

Los Angeles sprawls across a vast basin between the Pacific Ocean and the San Gabriel Mountains, a city of relentless sunshine, palm-lined boulevards, and dreams both realized and shattered. Beyond the Hollywood glamour, LA is a mosaic of distinct neighborhoods, each with its own character: the beachy vibe of Venice, the luxury of Beverly Hills, the creativity of Silver Lake, and the incredible diversity of food from every corner of the globe.

## Hollywood & Culture

The Hollywood Sign perches on Mount Lee, visible from across the city, a beacon of the entertainment industry that defines LA's identity. The Griffith Observatory offers planetarium shows and jaw-dropping views of the city and the Pacific. The Getty Center houses world-class art in a stunning Richard Meier building overlooking the city. LACMA's "Urban Light" installation, 202 restored street lamps, has become one of the city's most photographed landmarks.

## Beaches & Neighborhoods

Santa Monica Pier's Ferris wheel and arcade evoke classic California nostalgia, while the Venice Beach boardwalk is a carnival of street performers, muralists, and skateboarders. Malibu's coastline offers dramatic cliffs and pristine beaches. The food scene is extraordinary: Koreatown has more Korean restaurants than most neighborhoods on earth, Grand Central Market serves everything from pupusas to ramen, and roadside taco trucks serving al pastor are a legitimate culinary institution.
