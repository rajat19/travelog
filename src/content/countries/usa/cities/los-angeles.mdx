## A City of Neighborhoods

> LA doesn't have a center. It is a massive, sprawling collection of distinct neighborhoods connected by freeways and palm trees.

Los Angeles is often frustrating for first-time visitors because they try to treat it like a normal, centralized city. The secret to LA is treating it like a county-wide road trip. You don't "go to LA"—you go to Silver Lake for coffee, Santa Monica for the beach, and West Hollywood for dinner. 

<Highlight title="LA Highlights">
- **Griffith Observatory**: Watching the sun set over the sprawling grid of the city out to the Pacific Ocean.
- **The Getty Center**: Taking the tram up the hill to see world-class art and incredible white travertine architecture.
- **Venice Boardwalk**: The sheer bizarre, vibrant people-watching of muscle beach, skaters, and street performers.
</Highlight>

<Tip title="The Traffic Variable">
Always, always check Google Maps traffic before moving between neighborhoods. A drive that takes 20 minutes at 11 AM might take 90 minutes at 4:30 PM. Group your daily activities strictly by neighborhood to avoid spending your trip on the 405 freeway.
</Tip>

## Coastline & Canyons

LA's geography is its greatest asset. The Santa Monica mountains cut right through the city, offering incredible hiking (like Runyon Canyon or the trails behind the Hollywood Sign) just minutes from major urban centers. To the west, the Pacific Coast Highway offers one of the most iconic drives in the world through Malibu.

## The Food Scene

LA is arguably the most exciting food city in the US right now. Its proximity to Mexico and deep immigrant communities mean you will find the best tacos, Korean BBQ, and Thai food outside of their home countries.

<FoodGuide>

</FoodGuide>

<Itinerary>
- **Day 1: The Westside** — Rent a bike to ride from Santa Monica Pier to Venice Beach (Abbot Kinney Blvd for lunch).
- **Day 2: Art & Views** — The Getty Center in the morning → Sunset at Griffith Observatory.
- **Day 3: Hollywood & Eastside** — Quick look at the Walk of Fame → Hike Runyon Canyon → Dinner in Silver Lake or Los Feliz.
- **Day 4: Theme Parks or Coast** — Spend a day at Universal Studios Hollywood OR take a drive up the PCH to Malibu (El Matador State Beach).
</Itinerary>

## Logistics

<GettingThere>
Los Angeles International Airport (LAX) is notoriously chaotic. If you aren't renting a car, take the LAX-it shuttle to the ride-share lot to catch your Uber/Lyft. Alternatively, try to fly into Burbank (BUR) which is infinitely smaller and easier to navigate.
</GettingThere>

<Accommodation title="Where Stayed">
**West Hollywood (WeHo)**: Placed perfectly in the middle. It makes driving to the beaches (West) or downtown (East) equidistant. It's highly walkable by LA standards and packed with great food and bars.
</Accommodation>
