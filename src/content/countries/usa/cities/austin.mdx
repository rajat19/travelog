## Keep Austin Weird

> Austin feels like an anomaly in Texas—a blue speck in point-blank range of hill country, obsessed equally with breakfast tacos and software.

Austin has undergone massive changes over the last decade, transitioning from a sleepy college town into a booming tech hub. Despite the soaring glass towers downtown, the city desperately tries to hold onto its motto: "Keep Austin Weird." The result is a city where venture capitalists and musicians cross paths in line for world-class barbecue.

<Highlight title="Austin Must-Do's">
- **Barton Springs Pool**: Swimming in a massive, natural limestone pool filled with 68°F (20°C) spring water year-round.
- **Congress Avenue Bats**: Watching 1.5 million Mexican free-tailed bats emerge from under the bridge at dusk (March-November).
- **South Congress (SoCo)**: Strolling past cowboy boot stores, indie boutiques, and vintage shops with the Capitol building in the distance.
</Highlight>

<Tip title="BBQ Strategy">
Central Texas BBQ is religion here. The famous spots (like Franklin Barbecue) require lining up at 7:00 AM with lawn chairs and a cooler of beer. If you don't have hours to kill, Terry Black’s or Micklethwait Craft Meats offer incredible BBQ with much faster lines.
</Tip>

## The Live Music Capital

They aren't joking about the motto. Music pours out of almost every venue on 6th Street and East Austin. Whether it's the legendary Continental Club or a dive bar on the East side, the quality of local musicianship is staggering. While the "Dirty 6th" district is wild and suited for college parties, East 6th and Rainey Street offer more grown-up, craft-cocktail-driven music scenes.

<Itinerary>
- **Day 1: Classic Austin** — Walk South Congress → Lunch at Home Slice Pizza → See the Congress Bridge Bats at dusk.
- **Day 2: Nature & Music** — Swim at Barton Springs → Hike the Greenbelt → Evening drinks and music on East 6th St.
- **Day 3: BBQ Mission** — Wait in line for top-tier BBQ for lunch → Explore the Texas State Capitol.
- **Day 4: Hill Country** — Drive to the Hill Country (Driftwood/Wimberley) for wineries and Salt Lick BBQ.
</Itinerary>

## Logistics

<GettingThere>
Austin-Bergstrom International Airport (AUS) is incredibly close to downtown (about 15-20 mins). A rideshare (Uber/Lyft) is the best way to get to your accommodation.
</GettingThere>

<Accommodation title="Where I Stayed">
**East Austin**: The perfect mix of neighborhood charm, food trucks, and trendy bars. It's much quieter than staying downtown, but still highly walkable (by Texas standards, at least).
</Accommodation>
