## Why Taiwan?

Taiwan is the hidden gem of East Asia. It has the high-tech efficiency and convenience culture of Japan, mixed with the chaotic, vibrant street energy of Southeast Asia. It is also, arguably, the friendliest country in Asia. 

People come to Taiwan to eat, and for good reason. The entire culture is built around snacking, night markets, and incredible tea. 

## The Rhythm

The pace of Taiwan is dictated by two things: caffeine and night markets. The daytime is for beautiful hikes, temple visits, and exploring indie cafes (Taiwan's coffee scene is world-class). But the country truly wakes up when the sun goes down and the night markets spark to life.

<Tip title="The EasyCard is Everything">
Buy an EasyCard at the airport or any convenience store. It works on the Taipei MRT, buses country-wide, convenience stores, and even some vending machines. 
</Tip>

## What Surprised Me

How incredibly green and mountainous it is. Taipei is surrounded by lush green peaks, meaning you can take the subway to the end of the line, hike through a dense jungle to a dormant volcano, soak in a hot spring, and be back in the city center for a bowl of beef noodle soup by dinner.

## Planning Notes

Taiwan's weather can be tricky—it is prone to typhoons in the late summer, and winters in Taipei bring constant drizzle. Spring and autumn are the absolute sweet spots.

<Highlight title="Taiwan Essentials">
- **Receipt Lottery**: Don't throw away your receipts! Every receipt in Taiwan is a lottery ticket drawn every two months. You can donate them in boxes at convenience stores if you won't be around to check.
- **Trash Trucks Play Beethoven**: You won't see many trash cans. Instead, yellow garbage trucks drive through neighborhoods playing classical music, and residents come out to hand-deliver their sorted trash.
- **Incredible Safety**: Taiwan consistently ranks as one of the safest countries in the world. Leaving a laptop on a cafe table while you use the restroom is common practice.
</Highlight>
