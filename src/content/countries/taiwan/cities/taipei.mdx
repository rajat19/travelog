## The City of Snacks

> Taipei is a city that feels immediately comfortable, wrapping you in the steam of dumpling shops and the glow of neon signs.

Taipei sits in a basin surrounded by green mountains, making it a rare mega-city where nature is always within sight. It is a city that doesn't try too hard to be flashy; its charm lies in its livability, safety, and a deep-rooted obsession with good food.

<Highlight title="Top Taipei Experiences">
- **Night Markets**: Eating your way through Raohe or Shilin night market.
- **Taipei 101**: Looking up at (or looking down from) one of the world's most iconic skyscrapers.
- **Beitou Hot Springs**: Taking the MRT straight to geothermal paradise for a soak.
</Highlight>

<Tip title="Bubble Tea Rules">
Taiwan invented Boba. When ordering, you will be asked for your preferred ice level (Normal, Less, None) and sugar level (100%, 70%, 50%, 30%, 0%). Go for 50% sugar on your first try!
</Tip>

## Temples & History

Taipei blends history into its urban fabric seamlessly. The Longshan Temple in Wanhua district is always thick with incense smoke and worshippers tossing moon blocks to consult the gods. Meanwhile, the colossal Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall offers a massive, imposing plaza that has become the democratic heart of the city.

## The Food Scene

If there is a reason to book a flight to Taipei, it's the food. The city's night markets are legendary, offering everything from black pepper pork buns to the infamous stinky tofu.

<FoodGuide>
| Dish | Where to Try | Price |
|------|--------------|-------|
| **Beef Noodle Soup** | Lin Dong Fang | ~NT$160 |
| **Xiao Long Bao (Soup Dumplings)** | Din Tai Fung (The original on Xinyi) | ~NT$220 |

| **Bubble Tea** | Chun Shui Tang or 50 Lan | ~NT$50 |

</FoodGuide>

<Itinerary>
- **Day 1: The Classics** — Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall → Yongkang Street for lunch (Din Tai Fung) → Taipei 101 → Elephant Mountain for sunset views.
- **Day 2: Old Taipei** — Longshan Temple → Bopiliao Historical Block → Ximending (the "Harajuku of Taipei") → Shilin Night Market.
- **Day 3: Spa Day** — MRT to Xinbeitou → Soak in a public or private hot spring → Tamsui for sunset over the river.
- **Day 4: Day Trip East** — Train to Ruifang → Jiufen Old Street (spirited away vibes) → Shifen Waterfall (launching a paper lantern).
</Itinerary>

## Logistics

<GettingThere>
Taoyuan International Airport (TPE) is the main hub, connecting to Taipei Main Station via the Airport MRT in just 35 minutes (NT$150). Songshan Airport (TSA) is incredibly convenient right in the city center, but mostly handles regional flights from Japan/Korea/China.
</GettingThere>

<Accommodation title="Where I Stayed">
**Xinyi District / Da'an**: Great access to cafes, Taipei 101, and very clean. If you want more youth culture and neon lights, stay in **Ximending**. If you want transit convenience, stay near **Taipei Main Station**.
</Accommodation>
