## Why Malaysia?

Malaysia often flies under the radar compared to its neighbors, Thailand and Singapore, but it offers an incredible mix of both worlds. You get the staggering modern infrastructure of a wealthy nation alongside the sprawling, chaotic street food markets of Southeast Asia.

Its unique strength lies in its profound multiculturalism. The mix of Malay, Chinese, and Indian cultures creates a society where festivals, architecture, and most importantly, the food, are endlessly diverse.

## The Rhythm

Malaysia is heavily connected and very easy to navigate. The highways are excellent, the trains in KL are fast, and almost everyone speaks fantastic English. It's a country that feels instantly accessible but still deeply authentic.

<Tip title="The MDAC">
Don't forget to fill out the Malaysia Digital Arrival Card (MDAC) online within 3 days prior to your arrival. It's mandatory for almost all visitors and will save you time at immigration.
</Tip>

## What Surprised Me

The sheer quality of the Indian food. The "Mamak" stalls (Indian-Muslim eateries) are the heartbeat of late-night Malaysia. Sitting on a plastic stool at 2 AM eating *Roti Canai* and drinking *Teh Tarik* (pulled tea) is a core memory from the trip.

## Planning Notes

Malaysia is very affordable, often matching Thailand's prices but with slightly better infrastructure in cities. 

<Highlight title="Malaysia Essentials">
- **Getting Around**: Grab is headquartered in Malaysia and dominates the ride-hailing market. Download it before you land.
- **Alcohol is Expensive**: Because Malaysia is a Muslim-majority country, alcohol is heavily taxed. The exception is Langkawi, which is a duty-free island.
- **Dress Code**: While cities are cosmopolitan, it's polite to dress somewhat modestly when away from the beach resorts or when visiting mosques and temples.
</Highlight>
