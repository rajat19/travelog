## A Capital Built in Layers

Kuala Lumpur does not flatten itself into a single postcard. Glass towers rise behind mosque domes and colonial façades, while monorails, malls, markets, and food courts keep the whole city in motion. It is a capital that feels commercial and lived-in at the same time.

## Icons Beyond the Skyline

The city’s skyline is the obvious draw, but some of the most memorable stops sit just outside that frame. Batu Caves adds scale, color, and ritual to a day in the city, while the Sultan Abdul Samad Building brings back the mood of old Kuala Lumpur on Merdeka Square. Newer landmarks like Merdeka 118 show how quickly the city keeps redefining itself.

## Street Food After Dark

Kuala Lumpur is especially good after sunset. Jalan Alor fills with smoke, skewers, noodles, and plastic tables, and neighborhoods like Bukit Bintang stay bright and busy long into the night. It is one of those cities where a travel day can be built just by following your appetite.
