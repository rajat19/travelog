## The Pink City

> Jaipur feels cinematic. Peacocks wander through ornate marble palace courtyards, and massive yellow forts stand guard on the surrounding hills.

Jaipur forms one corner of India's famous "Golden Triangle" (along with Delhi and Agra). It was painted terracotta pink in 1876 to welcome the Prince of Wales, and the old city has maintained the color ever since. It's a city dedicated to royal extravagance, from the City Palace to the incredible jewelry and textile markets.

<Highlight title="Top Jaipur Experiences">
- **Amber Fort**: Hiking (or taking a jeep) up the rugged hills to this massive, honey-colored sandstone fortress complex.
- **Hawa Mahal (Palace of Winds)**: the stunning pink honeycomb facade built so royal women could observe street festivals unseen.
- **Jantar Mantar**: The largest stone astronomical observatory in the world, featuring a massive, accurate sundial.
</Highlight>

<Tip title="Shopping Strategy">
Jaipur is world-famous for block-printed textiles, blue pottery, and gemstones. Bapu Bazaar is great for tourist shopping, but haggle hard. Start at 40% of the quoted price.
</Tip>

## The Food Scene

Rajasthani food evolved in a dry desert climate, relying heavily on milk, ghee, and lentils rather than fresh green vegetables. It is incredibly rich.

<FoodGuide>
| Dish | Where to Try | Price |
|------|--------------|-------|
| **Laal Maas (Spicy Mutton)** | Handi Restaurant | ~₹500 |
| **Dal Bati Churma** | Chokhi Dhani | ~₹900 (Thali) |
| **Pyaz Kachori** | Rawat Mishtan Bhandar | ~₹50 |
| **Lassi in Kulhad (Clay cup)** | Lassiwala (MI Road) | ~₹70 |
</FoodGuide>

<Itinerary>
- **Day 1: The Old City** — City Palace in the morning → Jantar Mantar → View Hawa Mahal from the cafes across the street → Evening shopping in Bapu Bazaar.
- **Day 2: The Forts** — Head out of the city to Amber Fort early to beat the crowds → Stop at Jal Mahal (Water Palace) for photos on the way back → Nahargarh Fort for sunset over the entire city.
- **Day 3: The Culture** — Albert Hall Museum → Patrika Gate (immensely photogenic) → Dinner at an authentic Rajasthani Thali place.
</Itinerary>

## Logistics

<GettingThere>
Jaipur International Airport (JAI) is well-connected. However, if coming from Delhi, the easiest and fastest way is the Vande Bharat Express or Shatabdi train, which takes just over 4 hours.
</GettingThere>

<Accommodation title="Where I Stayed">
**Bani Park / C-Scheme**: Stay slightly outside the pink city walls. Heritage haweli (mansion) hotels in Bani Park offer gorgeous inner courtyards, pools, and a quiet retreat from the intense market traffic.
</Accommodation>
