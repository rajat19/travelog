## The French Riviera of the East

> Walking down to the rocky promenade, past bougainvillea draped over mustard-yellow colonial houses, you can almost forget you are in India.

Pondicherry (or Puducherry) was the largest French colony in India until 1954. The town is still sharply divided by a canal. On the east lies the "White Town" (the French Quarter), featuring grid-layout streets with French names, European-style bakeries, and heritage villas. On the west lies the bustling "Heritage Town" (the Tamil Quarter), full of vibrant Hindu temples and typical Indian street energy.

<Highlight title="Top Pondicherry Experiences">
- **White Town Wandering**: Renting a bicycle to ride through the grid of pastel French colonial villas and leafy courtyards.
- **Sri Aurobindo Ashram**: The spiritual center of the city, founded in 1926. A place of profound silence and meditation.
- **The Promenade**: Walking the long, rocky beachfront of Goubert Avenue at dusk when the road is closed to motorized vehicles.
</Highlight>

<Tip title="Auroville">
About 30 minutes outside of town lies Auroville, an experimental, international "universal township." The massive golden Matrimandir sphere in its center is spectacular. You must book a viewing pass days in advance if you want to go inside the viewing area.
</Tip>

## The Food Scene

The culinary scene is a brilliant fusion. You have access to deeply spicy Tamil/Chettinad cuisine seamlessly mixed with authentic French bakeries serving perfect croissants.

<FoodGuide>
| Dish | Where to Try | Price |
|------|--------------|-------|
| **Croissant & Coffee** | Coromandel Cafe or Baker Street | ~₹250 |
| **South Indian Thali** | Surguru | ~₹150 |
| **Wood-Fired Pizza** | Cafe des Arts | ~₹400 |
</FoodGuide>

<Itinerary>
- **Day 1: French Quarter** — Rent a bicycle → Explore White Town architecture → Sri Aurobindo Ashram → Evening walk on the Promenade Beach.
- **Day 2: The Experiment** — Drive out to Auroville → View the Matrimandir → Have lunch at an Auroville vegan cafe → Serenity Beach for sunset.
- **Day 3: Culture Clash** — Cross the canal to the Heritage Town to see the brilliant colors of the Arulmigu Manakula Vinayagar Temple → Souvenir shopping (pottery, incense).
</Itinerary>

## Logistics

<GettingThere>
Pondicherry has a tiny airport, but the vast majority of visitors fly into Chennai (MAA) and take a 3.5-hour taxi down the beautiful East Coast Road (ECR).
</GettingThere>

<Accommodation title="Where I Stayed">
**White Town**: You must absolutely stay inside the French Quarter. Booking a heritage hotel (an old restored colonial villa) with a courtyard puts you walking distance to the promenade and the best cafes.
</Accommodation>
