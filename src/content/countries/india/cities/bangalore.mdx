## The Silicon Valley

> Bangalore (Bengaluru) operates on a completely different frequency. It is the city of young ambition, where every coffee shop is packed with founders pitching startups over filter coffee.

Before it became the unquestioned tech capital of India, Bangalore was famously known as the "Garden City," due to its massive canopy of trees and consistently perfect weather (thanks to its elevation on the Deccan Plateau). While extreme development has caused legendary traffic bottlenecks, the city remains vibrant, highly cosmopolitan, and famous for having the best craft beer culture in India.

<Highlight title="Top Bangalore Experiences">
- **Cubbon Park & Lalbagh**: Walking through these massive, highly-maintained botanical gardens right in the center of the dense urban grid.
- **The Microbreweries**: Indiranagar and Koramangala are lined with massive, multi-story open-air craft breweries.
- **Vidhana Soudha**: The colossal, neo-Dravidian state legislature building that anchors the political center.
</Highlight>

<Tip title="The Traffic Warning">
The rumors about Bangalore traffic are entirely true. Do not plan an itinerary that requires you to cross the city north-to-south during the day. Stick to exploring one neighborhood (like Indiranagar or Koramangala) at a time.
</Tip>

## The Food Scene

While you have access to incredible international dining and vegan cafes, the cornerstone of Bangalore eating is the Darshini (fast-food) stalls serving hyper-fresh South Indian breakfast staples.

<FoodGuide>
| Dish | Where to Try | Price |
|------|--------------|-------|
| **Benne Masala Dosa** | CTR (Shri Sagar) or Vidyarthi Bhavan | ~₹80 |
| **Filter Coffee** | Brahmin's Coffee Bar | ~₹25 |
| **Craft Beer Flight** | Toit or Windmills Craftworks | ~₹500 |
</FoodGuide>

<Itinerary>
- **Day 1: The Green Core** — Morning walk in Cubbon Park → State Central Library → View the Vidhana Soudha → Bangalore Palace.
- **Day 2: Startups and Pints** — Explore the cafes and boutiques of Indiranagar 100 Ft Road → Evening brewery hopping.
- **Day 3: South Indian Heritage** — Morning Dosa at Vidyarthi Bhavan → Explore the historic Bull Temple (Dodda Basavana Gudi) → Lalbagh Botanical Garden.
</Itinerary>

## Logistics

<GettingThere>
Kempegowda International Airport (BLR) is an excellent facility, but it is located extremely far (often a 1.5 to 2-hour drive) from the city center. Plan your flights accordingly so you don't miss them entirely due to traffic.
</GettingThere>

<Accommodation title="Where I Stayed">
**Indiranagar / Koramangala**: These are the lifelines of the city's youth culture. You'll be within walking distance of the most famous eateries, breweries, and boutique shopping, avoiding cross-city traffic.
</Accommodation>
