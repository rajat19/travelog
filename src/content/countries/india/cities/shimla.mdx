## The Summer Capital

> Walking The Ridge in the evening, with the Gothic spires of Christ Church cutting into the fog, you understand why the British moved an entire empire here every summer.

Built across a towering ridge in the foothills of the Himalayas, Shimla is the quintessential Indian hill station. When the heat of Delhi became unbearable, the British Indian government would literally pack up files on horse-drawn carts and move the capital to these cool pine forests. Today, it remains a heavily visited escape from the plains.

<Highlight title="Top Shimla Experiences">
- **The Toy Train**: Riding the UNESCO-listed Kalka-Shimla narrow gauge railway up incredibly steep mountain gradients.
- **The Ridge & Mall Road**: Strolling the pedestrian-only core of the city, lined with heritage buildings and cafes.
- **Jakhu Temple**: Hiking up to the highest peak in Shimla to see the massive 108-foot orange Hanuman statue (watch out for the aggressive monkeys!).
</Highlight>

<Tip title="Pedestrian Rules">
The central core of Shimla (Mall Road and The Ridge) is strictly closed to all vehicular traffic. You will have to walk uphill from the lower taxi drops or use the public tourism elevators built into the cliffs. Pack good walking shoes.
</Tip>

## The Food Scene

While heavily catering to tourists with North Indian and Continental fare, you can find brilliant local Himachali food and legacy bakeries dating back to the Raj.

<FoodGuide>
| Dish | Where to Try | Price |
|------|--------------|-------|
| **Sita Ram's Chole Bhature** | Lakkar Bazaar | ~₹70 |
| **Plum Cake & Pastries** | Minchys or Trishool Bakery | ~₹100 |
| **Himachali Dham (Thali)** | Ashiana Goofa | ~₹300 |
</FoodGuide>

<Itinerary>
- **Day 1: The Heritage Core** — Walk Mall Road → Christ Church → The Ridge → Scandal Point → State Museum.
- **Day 2: The High Peaks** — Hike up to Jakhu Temple in the morning (carry a stick for monkeys) → Indian Institute of Advanced Study (former Viceregal Lodge).
- **Day 3: Day Trips** — Take a taxi to Kufri (for views and winter snow) or Mashobra (for quieter pine forest hikes).
</Itinerary>

## Logistics

<GettingThere>
The nearest major broad-gauge railway station is Kalka. From Kalka, you can take the famous 5-hour "Toy Train" up the mountain. Alternatively, regular buses and private taxis make the winding drive up from Chandigarh in about 3.5 hours.
</GettingThere>

<Accommodation title="Where I Stayed">
**Near Mall Road**: It is crucial to stay close to the Mall Road or The Ridge. If you book a hotel far down the valley, you will spend your entire trip waiting for taxis or hiking exhausting, steep steps just to get dinner.
</Accommodation>
