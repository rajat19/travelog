## A Tale of Two Cities

> Delhi is a city built upon cities. Every neighborhood hides an ancient tomb, a forgotten sultan's fort, or a colossal Mughal monument.

Delhi is practically split in two. Old Delhi revolves around the Red Fort and Jama Masjid—it is a labyrinth of narrow, chaotic alleyways dating back to the 1600s where the air is thick with spice. New Delhi, designed by the British, features sprawling tree-lined avenues, massive traffic circles, and grand political architecture. 

<Highlight title="Top Delhi Experiences">
- **Humayun's Tomb**: The magnificent 16th-century red sandstone and marble precursor to the Taj Mahal.
- **Chandni Chowk**: Braving the immense chaos of Old Delhi's famous market on a cycle rickshaw.
- **Qutub Minar**: Looking up at the towering 73-meter brick minaret built in the 12th century.
</Highlight>

<Tip title="The Delhi Metro">
Do not try to navigate Delhi traffic in a taxi during rush hour. The Delhi Metro is world-class, heavily air-conditioned, incredibly cheap, and stretches to every corner of the National Capital Region.
</Tip>

## The Food Scene

Delhi is arguably the culinary capital of India when it comes to rich, heavy, northern flavors. The street food (chaat) is legendary, and the Mughal-influenced curries are unparalleled.

<FoodGuide>
| Dish | Where to Try | Price |
|------|--------------|-------|
| **Chole Bhature** | Sita Ram Diwan Chand | ~₹100 |
| **Butter Chicken** | Gulati (Pandara Road) | ~₹900 |
| **Parathas** | Paranthe Wali Gali (Old Delhi) | ~₹150 |
| **Kebab Platters** | Karim's (Near Jama Masjid) | ~₹400 |
</FoodGuide>

<Itinerary>
- **Day 1: The Mughal Era** — Red Fort in the morning → cycle rickshaw through Chandni Chowk → Jama Masjid → Karim's for lunch.
- **Day 2: The Empires** — Humayun's Tomb → Lodhi Gardens (afternoon stroll) → India Gate at sunset.
- **Day 3: South Delhi History** — Qutub Minar complex → Hauz Khas Village (ruins and trendy cafes) → Lotus Temple.
- **Day 4: Modern & Spiritual** — Akshardham Temple (allow half a day, absolutely massive) → Shopping and dining at Connaught Place.
</Itinerary>

## Logistics

<GettingThere>
Indira Gandhi International Airport (DEL) is India's largest hub. The Airport Express Metro Line is the absolute best way to get to the city center via New Delhi Railway Station in just 20 minutes.
</GettingThere>

<Accommodation title="Where I Stayed">
**South Delhi (Hauz Khas or Safdarjung)**: South Delhi is generally greener, quieter, and wealthier. It’s full of great cafes and parks, making it a wonderful retreat from the intense chaos of Old and Central Delhi.
</Accommodation>
