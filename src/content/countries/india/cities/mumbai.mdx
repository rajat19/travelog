## The Maximum City

> Mumbai never stops. It is the financial capital of India, the home of Bollywood, and a city where billionaire skyscrapers tower directly over immense, sprawling slums.

Mumbai (formerly Bombay) is built on an archipelago of seven islands squeezed against the Arabian Sea. Space here is at an absolute premium, meaning everyone—from celebrities to street vendors—shares the same chaotic, beautiful sidewalks. South Mumbai (SoBo) retains towering Victorian Gothic and Art Deco buildings, while the northern suburbs house startup tech hubs and endless traffic.

<Highlight title="Top Mumbai Experiences">
- **The Gateway of India**: The massive basalt arch on the waterfront, opposite the iconic Taj Mahal Palace hotel.
- **Marine Drive (The Queen's Necklace)**: Sitting on the sweeping C-shaped promenade at sunset, watching the city light up.
- **Colaba Causeway**: Getting lost in the crowded, historic streets of South Mumbai, famous for street shopping, dive bars, and cafes.
</Highlight>

<Tip title="The Local Train">
Riding the Mumbai Local Train is a rite of passage, but do not attempt it during peak rush hour (8-10 AM, 6-8 PM) unless you want to be physically crushed. During off-peak hours, it is the absolute fastest way to bypass the city's legendary traffic jams.
</Tip>

## The Food Scene

Mumbai’s food is heavily influenced by the sea, Marathi traditions, Parsi cafes, and its status as a fast-paced working city—meaning the street food is incredibly efficient and heavily carbohydrate-focused.

<FoodGuide>
| Dish | Where to Try | Price |
|------|--------------|-------|
| **Vada Pav (The Bombay Burger)** | Aaram Vada Pav (CST) | ~₹20 |
| **Pav Bhaji** | Sardar Pav Bhaji (Tardeo) | ~₹150 |
| **Parsi Bun Maska/Keema** | Kyani & Co. (Marine Lines)| ~₹80/150 |
| **Seafood Thali** | Trishna or Mahesh Lunch Home | ~₹1000 |
</FoodGuide>

<Itinerary>
- **Day 1: Historic SoBo** — Gateway of India → Taj Mahal Palace → Walk through Colaba → Kala Ghoda Arts District → Sunset at Marine Drive.
- **Day 2: The Infrastructure** — See the stunning Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus (CST) → Dhobi Ghat (massive open-air laundry) → Drive across the Bandra-Worli Sea Link.
- **Day 3: The Suburbs** — Explore the trendy cafes and street murals of Bandra → Walk the Bandstand Promenade → Sunset at Juhu Beach.
- **Day 4: Elephanta Caves** — Take an hour-long ferry from the Gateway to Elephanta Island to see ancient, 5th-century rock-cut Shiva temples.
</Itinerary>

## Logistics

<GettingThere>
Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj International Airport (BOM) is located deep in the suburbs. A cab to South Mumbai via the Sea Link can take anywhere from 1 hour at midnight to 2.5 hours during rush hour.
</GettingThere>

<Accommodation title="Where I Stayed">
**Colaba / Fort (South Mumbai)**: This is where first-time visitors must stay. It has all the colonial history, great walkability, and prevents you from having to commute from the suburbs into the tourist center every day.
</Accommodation>
