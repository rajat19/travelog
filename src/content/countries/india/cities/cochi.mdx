## The Queen of the Arabian Sea

> Stepping into Fort Kochi feels like breathing out. The aggressive pace of North India fades into the rustling of coconut palms and the smell of cardamom.

For over 600 years, Kochi has drawn merchants from across the globe in search of spices. Because of this, the historic center (Fort Kochi) is a beautiful, deeply bizarre cultural mashup: you will find a 16th-century Portuguese church sitting next to a Jewish synagogue, near massive Chinese fishing nets operated by locals speaking Malayalam.

<Highlight title="Top Kochi Experiences">
- **Fort Kochi Streets**: Wandering past ancient spice warehouses converted into art cafes and boutique hotels.
- **Chinese Fishing Nets**: Watching fishermen haul up these massive, cantilevered wooden structures at sunset.
- **Kathakali Performance**: Witnessing the highly stylized, traditional Keralan dance-drama involving elaborate makeup and facial expressions.
</Highlight>

<Tip title="The Backwaters">
While Kochi is coastal, you must take a day trip (or overnight houseboat) south to **Alleppey** (Alappuzha) to drift slowly through the iconic, mirror-like Kerala backwaters.
</Tip>

## The Food Scene

Keralan food is distinct—it relies heavily on coconut, curry leaves, tamarind, and the incredibly fresh seafood caught right off the coast.

<FoodGuide>
| Dish | Where to Try | Price |
|------|--------------|-------|
| **Fish Moilee (Coconut Fish Curry)** | Oceanos Restaurant | ~₹400 |
| **Appam with Veg Stew** | Kashi Art Cafe | ~₹250 |
| **Fresh Catch BBQ** | Beachfront stalls near the Nets | ~₹500 |
</FoodGuide>

<Itinerary>
- **Day 1: Fort Kochi Heritage** — Mattancherry Palace (Dutch Palace) → Paradesi Synagogue → Jew Town shopping → St. Francis Church.
- **Day 2: Coastal Rhythms** — Chinese Fishing nets at dawn → Cafe hopping in Fort Kochi → Evening Kathakali dance show.
- **Day 3: The Water** — Take a full-day tour to the Alleppey backwaters on a traditional wooden canoe or houseboat.
</Itinerary>

## Logistics

<GettingThere>
Cochin International Airport (COK) is famously the world's first fully solar-powered airport. It's about an hour's drive from Fort Kochi. Alternatively, Ernakulam Junction is the main railway hub connecting to the rest of India.
</GettingThere>

<Accommodation title="Where I Stayed">
**Fort Kochi**: Don't stay in the modern, concrete city of Ernakulam. Cross the water and stay inside the heritage district of Fort Kochi. It's highly walkable, incredibly scenic, and full of beautiful restored colonial homestays.
</Accommodation>
