## Deep in the Himalayas

> Surrounded by towering peaks and the rushing waters of the Beas River, Manali transitions seamlessly from quiet, forested hikes to adrenaline-heavy adventure sports.

Manali sits near the northern end of the Kullu Valley. It is essentially split into two worlds. New Manali is a busy, commercial grid built around the Mall Road, catering heavily to domestic honeymooners. Old Manali, located uphill, is a backpacker's enclave filled with bohemian cafes, acoustic music, and travelers heading further up into the high-altitude deserts of Leh and Spiti.

<Highlight title="Top Manali Experiences">
- **Solang Valley**: An adventure hub just outside town offering paragliding, zorbing, and skiing (in winter).
- **Old Manali**: Trekking up the hill to drink hot cider in wooden cafes nestled in apple orchards.
- **Rohtang Pass/Atal Tunnel**: Driving through the mountains to cross into the barren, breathtaking Lahaul Valley.
</Highlight>

<Tip title="The Atal Tunnel">
The recently opened Atal Tunnel (the longest highway tunnel above 10,000 feet in the world) allows you to bypass the treacherous Rohtang Pass. You can now take a brilliant day trip to Sissu in the Lahaul Valley in just an hour.
</Tip>

## The Food Scene

Old Manali shines with an incredible array of cafe culture, serving everything from Israeli Shakshuka to wood-fired pizza and rich local trout.

<Itinerary>
- **Day 1: The Divide** — Arrive, walk the Mall Road in New Manali → Hike up to Old Manali → Hadimba Temple (wooden temple in the cedar forest).
- **Day 2: Adventure** — Head to Solang Valley for paragliding or winter sports → Vashisht Village to soak in the natural hot springs.
- **Day 3: The High Pass** — Take a full day trip either to Rohtang Pass (summer only) or through the Atal Tunnel to Sissu for incredible snowscapes.
- **Day 4: Cafe Hopping** — A slow day in Old Manali walking through apple orchards and relaxing in cafes.
</Itinerary>

## Logistics

<GettingThere>
There is no train station. You must take an overnight Volvo bus from Delhi or Chandigarh (approx 12-14 hours). The drive is incredibly winding. If you get motion sickness, pack medication. The nearest airport (Kullu-Bhuntar) is small and prone to massive weather delays.
</GettingThere>

<Accommodation title="Where I Stayed">
**Old Manali**: If you value a quiet, backpacker-centric atmosphere with incredible cafes and views, stay here. Avoid the Mall Road area unless you want to be in the middle of dense tourist crowds.
</Accommodation>
