## Cyberabad Meets the Nizams

> Hyderabad exists in two distinct eras. On one side of the city are 400-year-old minarets and pearl markets; on the other are campuses for Google, Microsoft, and Amazon.

Historically ruled by the Nizams—once among the wealthiest men on earth—Hyderabad’s old city is a dense, heavily Islamic cultural center. However, driving across the city to "HITEC City" feels like teleporting into the future. The transition from chaotic bazaars to endless glassy skyscrapers makes it one of India's most fascinating transitional cities.

<Highlight title="Top Hyderabad Experiences">
- **Charminar**: The iconic four-minaret monument standing right in the chaotic, pulsing center of the old city.
- **Golconda Fort**: A massive, largely ruined diamond-trading citadel built on a 400-foot granite hill just outside the city.
- **Chowmahalla Palace**: The stunningly opulent former seat of the Nizams, full of crystal chandeliers and vintage cars.
</Highlight>

<Tip title="The Pearl Market">
Because the Nizams were obsessed with pearls, Hyderabad remains one of the world's premier destinations to buy them. Head to the Laad Bazaar right next to the Charminar.
</Tip>

## The Food Scene

There is no debate: Hyderabad is the Biryani capital of the world. The Hyderabadi Dum Biryani is a masterful blend of rice, meat, and heavy spices sealed with dough and cooked slowly over coals.

<FoodGuide>
| Dish | Where to Try | Price |
|------|--------------|-------|
| **Mutton Dum Biryani** | Shadab or Paradise | ~₹350 |
| **Haleem (Ramadan only)** | Pista House | ~₹250 |
| **Irani Chai & Osmania Biscuits** | Nimrah Cafe (facing Charminar) | ~₹50 |
| **Double Ka Meetha** | Most local bakeries | ~₹80 |
</FoodGuide>

<Itinerary>
- **Day 1: The Core History** — Charminar (go early) → Drink Irani Chai at Nimrah → Explore Laad Bazaar → Chowmahalla Palace → Salar Jung Museum.
- **Day 2: The Citadel** — Spend half a day climbing Golconda Fort (the acoustics at the entry gate are magical) → Qutb Shahi Tombs.
- **Day 3: The New City & Lake** — See the towering monolithic Buddha statue in the middle of Hussain Sagar Lake → Shop in Jubilee Hills → Drive past the IT hubs of HITEC City.
</Itinerary>

## Logistics

<GettingThere>
Rajiv Gandhi International Airport (HYD) is an excellent, highly-rated modern airport about 45 minutes south of the city center. The Pushpak Airport Liner buses are a great, cheap way into town.
</GettingThere>

<Accommodation title="Where I Stayed">
**Banjara Hills / Jubilee Hills**: These are the upscale, central neighborhoods. They offer fantastic cafes and restaurants, while buffering you slightly from the intense traffic of the old city and the sterility of extreme west HITEC city.
</Accommodation>
