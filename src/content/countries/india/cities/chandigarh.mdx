## The City Beautiful

> After the chaos of typical Indian cities, Chandigarh feels like an architectural illusion: perfectly straight roads, massive roundabouts, and dedicated sectors.

Post-independence India needed a new capital for Punjab, and the government gave Swiss-French architect Le Corbusier a blank canvas. The result is Chandigarh, a city divided into a strict grid of "Sectors," heavily greened with broad tree-lined avenues and brutalist concrete civic buildings. It consistently ranks as one of the cleanest and happiest cities in India.

<Highlight title="Top Chandigarh Experiences">
- **Sukhna Lake**: The focal point of the city's leisure—a massive man-made lake perfect for sunset walks or morning jogs.
- **Rock Garden of Nek Chand**: A 40-acre bizarre, brilliant sculpture garden built entirely out of industrial and home waste (broken bangles, sinks, electrical parts) in secret by a local official.
- **The Capitol Complex**: Le Corbusier's UNESCO World Heritage brutalist masterpiece, including the High Court and the Open Hand Monument.
</Highlight>

<Tip title="The Grid System">
The city is divided into Sectors (from 1 to 60+). Notably, there is no Sector 13 (as Le Corbusier considered the number unlucky). Sector 17 is the pedestrianized commercial core. 
</Tip>

## The Food Scene

Positioned right at the intersection of Punjab and Haryana, the food heavily leans towards rich Punjabi dhabas and excellent craft breweries.

<FoodGuide>
| Dish | Where to Try | Price |
|------|--------------|-------|
| **Butter Chicken** | Pal Dhaba (Sector 28) | ~₹500 |
| **Craft Beer** | Sector 26 Microbreweries | ~₹300/pint |
| **Chole Bhature** | Sindhi Sweets (Sector 17) | ~₹120 |
</FoodGuide>

<Itinerary>
- **Day 1: The Master Plan** — Capitol Complex (book a free tour in advance) → Open Hand Monument → Walk the commercial plaza of Sector 17.
- **Day 2: Art and Nature** — The Rock Garden in the morning → Relax at Sukhna Lake → Evening dinner and drinks in the trendy Sector 26.
</Itinerary>

## Logistics

<GettingThere>
Chandigarh International Airport (IXC) serves most domestic routes. It is also an easy 4-hour drive up the NH44 highway from Delhi, making it a very popular weekend road trip.
</GettingThere>

<Accommodation title="Where I Stayed">
**Sector 17 or Sector 22**: These sectors put you right in the center of the grid, with immediate walking access to the main shopping plazas and restaurants.
</Accommodation>
