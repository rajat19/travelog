## The City of Tehzeeb

> Lucknow moves differently than other northern Indian cities. The legacy of the Nawabs left behind a culture obsessed with politeness, poetry, and perfect slow-cooked food.

Lucknow is built entirely around "Tehzeeb" (etiquette/culture). The Nawabs (rulers) of Awadh were massive patrons of the arts, pouring their wealth into monumental architecture, classical music, and the culinary arts. Today, the city is a living museum of that golden age, most famous across India for producing the finest kebabs in the subcontinent.

<Highlight title="Top Lucknow Experiences">
- **Bara Imambara**: Getting gloriously lost inside the Bhool Bhulaiya, a massive, unsupported ceiling maze built above a grand congregation hall.
- **Rumi Darwaza**: The towering Turkish Gate that serves as the iconic symbol of the city.
- **The British Residency**: A haunting, preserved set of ruins from the 1857 siege, set in quiet, manicured lawns.
</Highlight>

<Tip title="The Chikankari Markets">
Lucknow is famous for "Chikankari" white-thread embroidery. Head to the bustling, incredibly dense Chowk area to buy authentic hand-embroidered Kurtas.
</Tip>

## The Food Scene

If you eat meat, Lucknow is arguably the best destination in India. Awadhi cuisine is famous for *Dum Pukht*—cooking meat incredibly slowly over low fire in sealed pots until it melts.

<FoodGuide>
| Dish | Where to Try | Price |
|------|--------------|-------|
| **Galouti Kebab/Tunday Kebab** | Tunday Kababi (Aminabad/Chowk) | ~₹100 |
| **Awadhi Biryani** | Idris Biryani or Wahid Biryani | ~₹200 |
| **Prakash Kulfi** | Prakash Kulfi (Aminabad) | ~₹70 |
| **Makhan Malai** | Chowk Street Vendors (Winter only) | ~₹50 |
</FoodGuide>

<Itinerary>
- **Day 1: Nawab History** — Bara Imambara & Bhool Bhulaiya → Rumi Darwaza → Chota Imambara → Evening food crawl in Chowk for Tunday Kebabs.
- **Day 2: Colonial Echoes** — The British Residency ruins → Hazratganj for shopping and modern cafe culture → Ambedkar Memorial Park in the evening.
</Itinerary>

## Logistics

<GettingThere>
Chaudhary Charan Singh International Airport (LKO) connects well domestically. From Delhi, the Shatabdi Express train takes about 6.5 hours and is very comfortable.
</GettingThere>

<Accommodation title="Where I Stayed">
**Hazratganj / Gomti Nagar**: Hazratganj is the central Victorian-style shopping district, offering great walkability. Gomti Nagar is the expansive modern district with high-end hotels and massive parks.
</Accommodation>
