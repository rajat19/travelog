## Why Indonesia?

Indonesia is impossibly vast—an archipelago of over 17,000 islands spanning a distance equivalent to New York to London. While Bali draws the vast majority of international visitors with its unique Hindu culture and boutique cafes, the rest of the country holds active volcanoes, dense jungles, and untouched diving reefs. 

## The Rhythm

Island time is real here. Infrastructure varies wildly depending on where you are. In Bali or Jakarta, you have modern conveniences and ride-hailing apps at your fingertips. Take a boat to the Nusa Islands or Lombok, and the pace of life slows to a crawl. You learn to embrace the relaxed, fluid nature of plans here.

<Tip title="The Power of WhatsApp">
WhatsApp is the absolute core of communication in Indonesia. You'll use it to message your hotel, book a driver, order a custom coffee, and arrange boat tickets. Get a local Telkomsel eSIM so you always have data.
</Tip>

## Planning Notes

Because Indonesia is spread across the equator, its seasons are opposite to mainland Southeast Asia. When Thailand and Vietnam are dealing with monsoon rains (June-August), Indonesia is experiencing its dry, sunny high season.

<Highlight title="Indonesia Essentials">
- **Cash is Still Crucial**: While upscale spots take cards, you need millions of Rupiah in cash for local warungs, drivers, and small island economies.
- **Modesty Outside Bali**: Bali is predominantly Hindu and very accustomed to tourists in beachwear. The rest of Indonesia is predominantly Muslim; dress modestly (covering shoulders and knees) when leaving Bali.
- **Blue Bird Taxis**: If you must take a taxi instead of a Gojek/Grab, always insist on a metric "Blue Bird" taxi to avoid being overcharged.
</Highlight>
