## The City That Never Sleeps

> Pattaya is notorious for its nightlife, but beyond Walking Street lies a massive coastal city trying to rebrand itself with luxury resorts and family attractions.

Pattaya sits just two hours southeast of Bangkok, making it the most accessible beach city for weekend escapes. Historically known primarily as a raucous adult playground centered around "Walking Street," the city has grown exponentially. While the chaotic nightlife is still very much there, it now sits alongside massive waterparks, enormous shopping malls, and bizarre architectural wonders.

<Highlight title="Beyond the Neon">
- **Sanctuary of Truth**: A massive, intricate, all-wooden monument built entirely without metal nails, sitting right on the ocean.
- **Koh Larn (Coral Island)**: A 45-minute ferry ride escapes the murky city water for pristine, turquoise beaches.
- **Nong Nooch Garden**: An absolutely massive, slightly bizarre botanical garden complete with dinosaur statues and incredible landscaping.
</Highlight>

<Tip title="Beach Warning">
Do not expect pristine, swimmable water at Pattaya Beach in the city center. It's crowded with speedboats and jet skis. If you want a proper beach day, take the cheap ferry from Bali Hai pier to Koh Larn.
</Tip>

## The Food Scene

Because Pattaya targets expats and tourists from across the globe, you can find incredibly authentic Indian, Russian, Middle Eastern, and European food right next to Thai street carts.

<FoodGuide>
| Dish | Where to Try | Price |
|------|--------------|-------|
| **Som Tum (Papaya Salad)** | Somtum Amphon | ~฿60 |
| **Fresh Seafood** | Mum Aroi (Naklua) | ~฿300+ |
| **Middle Eastern** | Soi Arab (South Pattaya) | ~฿150-300 |
</FoodGuide>

<Itinerary>
- **Day 1: Architecture & Views** — Visit the Sanctuary of Truth in the morning → Relax at a beach club in Wongamat → Experience the chaos of Walking Street at night.
- **Day 2: Island Escape** — Take the 9:00 AM ferry to Koh Larn → Rent a scooter to explore Tawaen Beach → Head back for sunset at the Pattaya Viewpoint.
</Itinerary>

## Logistics

<GettingThere>
A bus from Bangkok's Ekkamai terminal takes about 2.5 hours and costs less than ฿150. A private taxi directly from Suvarnabhumi Airport (BKK) takes under 2 hours and costs roughly ฿1,500.
</GettingThere>

<Accommodation title="Where I Stayed">
**Wongamat / Naklua (North Pattaya)**: If you want to avoid the chaotic bar scene and constant traffic, stay in the North. It has nicer, quieter beaches and feels much more like a proper holiday resort area.
</Accommodation>
