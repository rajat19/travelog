## Gateway to the Golden Triangle

Chiang Rai sits in the mountainous north of Thailand, where the borders of Thailand, Laos, and Myanmar meet. It's a world away from Bangkok: quieter, cooler, and surrounded by misty green mountains. The city has reinvented itself as an art capital, home to some of Thailand's most extraordinary modern temples.

## The White Temple

Wat Rong Khun, the White Temple, is unlike anything else in Thailand or the world. Designed by artist Chalermchai Kositpipat, it's a blinding vision of white and mirrored glass, representing the purity of Buddha. The bridge to the entrance is flanked by hundreds of reaching hands, symbolizing desire. Inside, the murals are shockingly contemporary, featuring superheroes, spaceships, and pop culture icons alongside traditional Buddhist imagery.

## Blue Temple & Beyond

The Blue Temple, or Wat Rong Suea Ten, is the White Temple's stunning counterpart: deep sapphire blues adorned with gold, housing a brilliant white Buddha. Beyond the temples, Chiang Rai offers treks through hill tribe villages, tea plantations in Doi Mae Salong, and Singha Park, a massive agricultural park with sweeping mountain views. The night bazaar is a treasure trove of handcrafted goods, tribal textiles, and northern Thai delicacies.
