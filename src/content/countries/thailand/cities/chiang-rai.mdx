## The Artistic North

> Chiang Rai is deeply tied to its artists. The architecture here tells a different, more contemporary story than the ancient ruins of the south.

Chiang Rai is often visited just as a day trip from Chiang Mai, but giving it a few days allows you to dive into the unique convergence of art and nature. Situated near the borders of Myanmar and Laos (the Golden Triangle), it has a distinctly slower, cooler vibe than the rest of the country.

<Highlight title="Top Chiang Rai Experiences">
- **The White Temple (Wat Rong Khun)**: An incredible, surreal contemporary art exhibit in the style of a Buddhist temple.
- **The Blue Temple (Wat Rong Suea Ten)**: A mesmerizing, intensely blue temple interior with stunning modern murals.
- **The Black House (Baan Dam Museum)**: Dark, gothic-style wooden structures filled with animal remains and avant-garde art.
</Highlight>

<Tip title="Getting Around">
Public transit is sparse. You absolutely need to rent a scooter or hire a local driver (via Grab or your hotel) for the day, as the main attractions are spread far outside the city center.
</Tip>

## The Golden Triangle

A drive an hour north takes you to the Golden Triangle, the point on the Mekong River where Thailand, Laos, and Myanmar meet. It was historically famous for opium production but is now a peaceful viewpoint with a fascinating (and mildly tragic) museum dedicated to the region's history with the drug trade (The Hall of Opium).

## The Food Scene

Northern Thai food is heavily influenced by neighboring Myanmar and lacks the heavy coconut curries of the south. 

<FoodGuide>
| Dish | Where to Try | Price |

| **Nam Prik Ong (Pork/Tomato Dip)** | Lu Lam Restaurant | ~฿100 |
</FoodGuide>

<Itinerary>
- **Day 1: The Colors** — White Temple in the morning → Singha Park → Blue Temple in the late afternoon.
- **Day 2: The Dark & The Views** — The Black House (Baan Dam) → Choui Fong Tea Plantation (great for lunch and matcha).
- **Day 3: The Border** — Drive up to the Golden Triangle → Hall of Opium Museum → Mae Sai border town.
</Itinerary>

## Logistics

<GettingThere>
Chiang Rai International Airport (CEI) has incredibly cheap, frequent 1-hour flights from Bangkok. Alternatively, it's a 3-4 hour bus ride from Chiang Mai through the mountains.
</GettingThere>

<Accommodation title="Where I Stayed">
**City Center (Near Clock Tower)**: It's small and quiet, but places you near the Night Bazaar for dinner and the beautifully lit golden clock tower (designed by the same artist who built the White Temple).
</Accommodation>
