## The Rose of the North

> Life inside the old city moat feels like a village that happens to have the best coffee in Southeast Asia.

Chiang Mai is vastly different from Bangkok. Surrounded by mountains and lush jungles, the city is famous for its relaxed pace, hundreds of ancient temples, and a massive community of expats and digital nomads. It's the kind of place where you plan to stay for three days and end up staying for three weeks.

<Highlight title="Top Chiang Mai Experiences">
- **Doi Suthep**: Taking a songthaew (red truck) up the winding mountain road to the glowing golden temple overlooking the city.
- **Sunday Walking Street**: The massive, chaotic, and brilliant weekend market running right through the heart of the old city.
- **Elephant Nature Park**: Spending a day feeding and bathing rescued elephants at an ethical, riding-free sanctuary.
</Highlight>

<Tip title="The Burning Season">
**Crucial Warning:** Avoid Chiang Mai from late February through April. It is the "smoky season" or "burning season" when farmers in the surrounding mountains burn their crops. The air quality reaches hazardous levels, and visibility drops to zero.
</Tip>

## Temples & Moats

The center of Chiang Mai is a perfect square, surrounded by an ancient brick wall and a water-filled moat. Inside, you can barely walk two blocks without stumbling into a 500-year-old temple. *Wat Chedi Luang*, a massive, partially ruined stupa in the center of town, is breathtaking at night. 

## The Food & Cafe Scene

Chiang Mai has a coffee scene that rivals Melbourne or Seattle. Neighborhoods like Nimman are packed with aesthetic cafes, coworking spaces, and incredible street food.

<FoodGuide>
| Dish | Where to Try | Price |
|------|--------------|-------|
| **Khao Soi (The Icon)** | Khao Soi Mae Sai or Khun Yai | ~฿50 |
| **Pork Leg Rice (Khao Kha Moo)** | The Cowboy Hat Lady (North Gate) | ~฿60 |
| **Specialty Coffee** | Akha Ama Coffee | ~฿70 |
| **Fancy Brunch** | Rustic & Blue (Nimman) | ~฿250+ |
</FoodGuide>

<Itinerary>
- **Day 1: Old City Wandering** — Explore the moat, Wat Phra Singh, Wat Chedi Luang → Grab dinner at the South Gate night market.
- **Day 2: The Mountain** — Hike the Monk's Trail to Wat Pha Lat → Continue up to Doi Suthep for sunset.
- **Day 3: The Sanctuary** — Full day trip to Elephant Nature Park (book months in advance!).
- **Day 4: Cafe Culture & Markets** — Explore Nimmanhaemin Road cafes → Sunday Walking Street (if Sunday).
</Itinerary>

## Logistics

<GettingThere>
Chiang Mai International Airport (CNX) is wonderfully close to the city center—just a 10-15 minute ride. The overnight sleeper train from Bangkok is a classic backpacker experience that takes 13 hours but is highly comfortable.
</GettingThere>

<Accommodation title="Where I Stayed">
**The Old City**: Staying inside the moat gives you the most authentic feel and easy walking access to the major temples and weekend markets. If you want a more modern, expat-heavy vibe, stay in **Nimman**.
</Accommodation>
