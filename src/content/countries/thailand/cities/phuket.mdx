## The Pearl of the Andaman

> Phuket is massive. It’s less of an isolated island and more of a heavily developed province that happens to have world-class beaches.

Phuket gets a mixed reputation due to overtourism in parts, but the island is so large that you can curate exactly the trip you want. If you want relentless nightlife and packed beaches, Patong delivers. If you want ultra-luxury resorts, Bang Tao is perfect. And if you want culture and history, Phuket Old Town is a hidden gem.

<Highlight title="Top Phuket Experiences">
- **Phang Nga Bay**: Taking a boat trip through the towering limestone karsts (James Bond Island).
- **Phuket Old Town**: Wandering the Sino-Portuguese architecture and colorful shophouses.
- **The Big Buddha**: Driving up the Nakkerd Hills to see the massive 45-meter marble Buddha with 360-degree island views.
</Highlight>

<Tip title="Transport Monopoly">
Taxis and tuk-tuks in Phuket operate like a mafia and are significantly more expensive than the rest of Thailand. Download the **InDrive** or **Bolt** apps for much cheaper rides, though you might have to walk a block away from the taxi stands to get picked up.
</Tip>

## Finding Your Beach

The west coast holds the best beaches. *Kata* and *Karon* are family-friendly and beautiful. *Patong* is loud, completely chaotic, and deeply commercialized. For quieter sands, head south to *Nai Harn* or far north to *Mai Khao* where planes fly directly over your head before landing.

## The Food Scene

Phuket Old Town has incredible southern Thai food, which is notoriously spicy and relies heavily on turmeric and seafood. 

<FoodGuide>
| Dish | Where to Try | Price |
|------|--------------|-------|
| **Pad Kra Pao Seafood** | Mor Mu Dong (East Coast) | ~฿150 |
| **Massaman Curry** | Blue Elephant (Old Town) | ~฿600 |
| **Moo Hong (Braised Pork)** | Raya Restaurant (Old Town) | ~฿250 |
| **Fresh Seafood BBQ** | Rawai Seafood Market | Varies by weight |
</FoodGuide>

<Itinerary>
- **Day 1: The South** — Big Buddha in the morning → Wat Chalong → Sunset at Promthep Cape.
- **Day 2: The Bay** — Full day boat tour out to Phang Nga Bay and the floating Muslim village of Koh Panyee.
- **Day 3: Beach Hopping** — Rent a scooter (if experienced) and hop from Kata to Nai Harn.
- **Day 4: History & Culture** — Spend the afternoon exploring the cafes in Phuket Old Town → Sunday Walking Street Market.
</Itinerary>

## Logistics

<GettingThere>
Phuket International Airport (HKT) is located in the far north of the island. Getting to the popular southern beaches (like Kata or Patong) takes an hour by taxi and will cost around ฿800-1000. 
</GettingThere>

<Accommodation title="Where I Stayed">
**Kata Beach**: It offers the best balance. The beach is stunning, the water is clear, there are plenty of restaurants, but it lacks the overwhelming chaos of Patong.
</Accommodation>
