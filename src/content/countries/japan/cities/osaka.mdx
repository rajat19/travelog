## Japan's Kitchen

Osaka is often called "Japan's Kitchen" (`tenka no daidokoro`), and for good reason. This is a city that takes food more seriously than almost anywhere else on earth. The phrase `kuidaore`, or eat until you drop, is the city's unofficial motto. Osakans are known throughout Japan for their warmth, humor, and frankness, a refreshing contrast to Tokyo's polished reserve.

## Dotonbori & Nightlife

Dotonbori is Osaka's neon-soaked entertainment district, famous for the giant mechanical crab, the running Glico Man sign, and endless restaurants lining the canal. This is where you eat takoyaki from street vendors, slurp ramen at 2 AM, and watch the city's electric nightlife unfold. The nearby Shinsekai district has a retro-futuristic charm with its Tsutenkaku Tower and kushikatsu shops.

## Osaka Castle & Culture

Osaka Castle sits in a park surrounded by moats and stone walls, its white and gold exterior gleaming above the cherry trees. The castle museum inside chronicles the dramatic life of Toyotomi Hideyoshi, who unified Japan. The Sumiyoshi Taisha shrine, one of Japan's oldest, features distinctive architecture that predates Chinese influence. For a unique experience, catch a performance at the National Bunraku Theatre, Japan's traditional puppet drama.
