## Kuidaore: Eat Until You Drop

> If Tokyo is reserved and polite, Osaka is loud, gregarious, and obsessed with food. 

Osaka is informally known as the "kitchen of Japan." Built around a culture of *kuidaore* (eat until you drop), the city's energy is infectious. People here walk a little faster, talk a little louder, and the street food culture is the vibrant heart of the city. Walking down Dotonbori at night under the glow of the famous Glico Man sign is an essential Japanese experience.

<Highlight title="Top Things to Do">
- **Dotonbori at Night**: Taking in the massive, moving neon signs (giant crabs, dragons, sushi).
- **Osaka Castle**: Exploring the beautiful grounds and the moats of this reconstructed historic icon.
- **Shinsekai**: Stepping back into a retro-futuristic version of the 1900s, eating *kushikatsu* under the Tsutenkaku Tower.
</Highlight>

<Tip title="Escalator Etiquette">
Unlike Tokyo where everyone stands on the left side of the escalator, in Osaka (and much of the Kansai region), you stand on the **right** side and walk on the left.
</Tip>

## The Soul Food of Kansai

Osaka is the birthplace of some of Japan's most famous street foods. You can't leave without eating Takoyaki (octopus balls) burning hot straight off the grill, or Okonomiyaki (savory cabbage pancakes). 

<FoodGuide>
| Dish | Where to Try | Price |
|------|--------------|-------|
| **Takoyaki** | Wanaka (Namba) | ¥500 for 8 |
| **Okonomiyaki** | Mizuno (Dotonbori) | ¥1,500 |
| **Kushikatsu (Fried Skewers)** | Daruma (Shinsekai) | ¥150/skewer |
| **Rikuro's Cheesecake** | Namba Station | ¥900/cake |
</FoodGuide>

<Itinerary>
- **Day 1: The Main Event** — Osaka Castle in the morning → Shinsaibashi shopping → Evening street food crawl through Dotonbori.
- **Day 2: Retro Osaka** — Shinsekai for lunch (Kushikatsu) → Hozenji Yokocho (moss-covered temple hidden in alleys) → Umeda Sky Building for sunset views.
- **Day 3: Thrills or Trips** — Spend the day at Universal Studios Japan (Super Nintendo World!) OR take a quick train down to Kuromon Ichiba Market before a day trip to Nara to see the deer.
</Itinerary>

## Logistics

<GettingThere>
Kansai International Airport (KIX) is the main entry point to the region. The Nankai Rapi:t train will get you to Namba Station in about 35 minutes. If coming from Tokyo, the Shinkansen takes about 2.5 hours to Shin-Osaka station.
</GettingThere>

<Accommodation title="Where Stayed">
**Namba / Dotonbori area** is where you want to be. It puts you in walking distance of the best food and nightlife, and Namba is a massive transit hub connecting to the airport and other cities.
</Accommodation>
