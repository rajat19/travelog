## Where Past Meets Future

Tokyo is a city of impossible contrasts. In the span of a single block, you can move from a 400-year-old shrine to a building covered in holographic screens. The world's largest metropolitan area runs with clockwork precision: trains arrive to the second, convenience stores stock gourmet meals, and every detail of daily life has been refined into an art form.

## Shibuya & Shinjuku

Shibuya Crossing is the world's busiest intersection, with up to 3,000 people crossing simultaneously when the lights change in a mesmerizing dance of organized chaos. Above it, the Shibuya Sky observation deck offers a 360-degree view of the Tokyo sprawl stretching to the horizon. Shinjuku's neon-drenched streets lead to Golden Gai, six narrow alleys lined with over 200 tiny bars, each seating just a handful of people.

## Temples & Tradition

Senso-ji in Asakusa is Tokyo's oldest temple, its Thunder Gate draped with an enormous red lantern. The approach through Nakamise-dori market has been selling traditional snacks and souvenirs for centuries. In Meiji Jingu, a forest of 100,000 trees creates a pocket of peace in the heart of the city. Tokyo Tower and the newer Skytree punctuate the skyline, offering views of Mount Fuji on clear days.
