## The Soul of Japan

Kyoto was Japan's capital for over a thousand years, and its 2,000 temples, 17 UNESCO World Heritage sites, and living traditions make it the cultural heart of the nation. Here, geisha still glide through twilight streets, tea ceremonies follow centuries-old rituals, and bamboo groves create otherworldly corridors of green. Kyoto rewards slow travel: every neighborhood reveals hidden gardens, tiny shrines, and artisan workshops.

## Fushimi Inari & Bamboo Grove

Fushimi Inari Taisha is an iconic trail of ten thousand vermilion torii gates winding up a mountain. Walking through the tunnel of gates as dappled light filters through is a transcendent experience. The Arashiyama Bamboo Grove is equally magical: towering stalks of bamboo create a cathedral-like space where the main soundtrack is the wind moving through the canopy. Both sites are free to visit and unforgettable.

## Golden Pavilion & Gardens

Kinkaku-ji, the Golden Pavilion, is Japan's most famous sight: a three-story structure covered entirely in gold leaf, perfectly reflected in the surrounding mirror pond. Each floor represents a different architectural style, symbolizing the harmony of different cultures. Nearby, the Ryoan-ji rock garden offers fifteen carefully placed stones on raked white gravel, a masterpiece of Zen minimalism that has inspired contemplation for over 500 years.
